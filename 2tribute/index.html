Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

The People's President & Missile Man of India

About Dr. Kalam

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. He was an Indian aerospace scientist and politician who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He played a pivotal role in India's civilian space program and military missile development, earning him the nickname "Missile Man of India."

Dr. Kalam was not only a scientist and leader but also a humble teacher who believed in igniting young minds. His dedication to education, innovation, and national development left a lasting impact.

His Legacy

Dr. Kalam's vision for a developed India was outlined in his book "India 2020." He inspired millions through his speeches, books, and work. Even after his presidency, he continued to reach out to students and youth across the country.

He passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong. His life remains a beacon of hope and inspiration for future generations.
